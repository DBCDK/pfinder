Simple result with two records

<result>
  <hitCount>20</hitCount>
  <collecion>
    <object>
      <title>Anders</title>
    </object>
    <object>
      <title>Benny</title>
    </object>
  </collecion>
</result>

Tag content is put in $, repetitve tags in arrays:

{"result":{"hitcount":{"$":"20"},"collection":{"object":[{"title":{"$":"Anders"}},{"title":{"$":"Benny"}}]}}}

Two records with attributes

<result>
  <hitCount>20</hitCount>
  <collecion>
    <object>
      <title lingo="danishName">Anders</title>
    </object>
    <object>
      <title lingo="englishName">Bart</title>
    </object>
  </collecion>
</result>

Attributes are prefixed wih @ and placed as a (sub)-tag value:

{"result":{"hitcount":{"$":"20"},"collection":{"object":[{"title":{"$":"Anders","@lingo":{"$":"danishName"}}},{"title":{"$":"Bart","@lingo":{"$":"englishName"}}}]}}}

Two records with attributes and namespace

<result xmlns:dc="http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/">
  <hitCount>20</hitCount>
  <collecion>
    <object>
      <dc:title lingo="danishName">Anders</title>
    </object>
    <object>
      <dc:title lingo="englishName">Bart</title>
    </object>
  </collecion>
</result>

Namespace-prefix is put in @ and @namespaces are added the end:

{"result":{"hitcount":{"$":"20"},"collection":{"object":[{"title":{"$":"Anders","@lingo":{"$":"danishName"},"@":"dc"}},{"title":{"$":"Bart","@lingo":{"$":"englishName"},"@":"dc"}}]}},"@namespaces":{"dc":"http:\/\/purl.org\/dc\/elements\/1.1\/"}}
